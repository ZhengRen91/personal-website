About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou). My research lies at the intersection of GIScience, urban morphology, and geospatial big data, with a focus on the living structure of cities — how urban form, function, and human activity organize across scales.

I received my PhD from the University of Gävle, Sweden, under the supervision of Prof. Bin Jiang and Prof. Stefan Seipel. My doctoral work explored how geographic space behaves as a living structure and how this perspective can be operationalized through geospatial big data to better understand cities and human activity.

I am always open to collaborations on urban analytics, living structure theory, and data-driven studies of cities. Feel free to reach out by email.

Research Interests

  • Urban Morphology & Living Structure — scaling laws, head/tail breaks, hierarchical urban form.
  • Geospatial Big Data & GIScience — POI, building footprints, nighttime light, street networks.
  • Urban Centers & Human Activity — topology-based identification, data-driven prediction.
